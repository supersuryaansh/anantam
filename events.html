/// ANANT NETRUNN

The 4-Day Hackathon is the centre-piece of ANANTAM 2023-24. It's an intensive coding competition where participants have four days to collaborate, innovate, and create real-world solutions using technology

Rules

  • Teams consist of a maximum of four individuals. Each team must have a minimum of two members.
  • Each team will be presented with a set of real-world challenges or themes to choose from in two categories, Hardware and Software.
  • The responsibility of the components and the materials required for developing the prototype during the “ANANT NETRUNN” Hackthon lies solely on the participating teams.
  • Clearly communicate the email-ids and mobile numbers of all team members as well
  • The Team Name should be unique and not contain the name of your institute in any form
  • No participant shall engage in any malicious/illegal activities which do not adhere to the general code of conduct for developers. Participants will be immediately disqualified if they are found indulging in malpractice.
  • Teams can use libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.
  • Teams should be made up exclusively of students (or recent graduates within one year of having graduated) who are not organizers, volunteers, judges, sponsors, or in any other privileged position at the event.
  • No reimbursement/travel allowance will be provided by ANANTAM.
  • Participants have to bear their own responsibility to safeguard their belongings during the event.
  • Teams are expected to develop a working prototype of the solution within the 4-day duration. For the Hardware category, participants need to create a physical prototype.
  • In case of a tie, the decision of Judges will be final and binding.
  • Organizers reserve the right to interpret and enforce the rules as they see fit. Decisions made by organizers are final.

/// ROBOWAR

The Robo-Battle is an exciting robotics competition where teams design and build combat robots that engage in thrilling battles within an arena. This event tests engineering skills, strategy, and teamwork. The event is spans across two days with the first day featuring a Robo-race and day two brings the epic showdown battle. The Robo-Battle emphasizes practical application of engineering skills, teamwork, and strategic thinking, aligning with our college's commitment to fostering technical excellence.

Task

  • For Robo Race: Design a robot either wired or wireless within the specified dimensions that can operated manually and can travel through all turns of the track. The robot that will complete the specified task in least time will be the winner. Think your robot can overcome any obstacle-big or small in the least of time.
  • For Robo War:The platform will be a circular raised area with a diameter of 3 meters. Pushing an opponent out of the arena, flipping an opponent, or scoring points by hitting specific targets. Each match consists of multiple rounds depending upon judges.

Rules

  • Teams consist of a maximum of four individuals. Each team must have a minimum of two members.
  • The maximum dimension of the robot can be 35 cm x 25cm x20 cm (l x b x h).
  • The robot may be wired or wireless
  • Maximum weight must not exceed 8 kg. The participants will be provided with 220 Volts, 50Hz standard AC supply. Participants will have to themselves arrange for adaptor or batteries.
  • The machine must not be made from Lego parts, or any ready-made kit, if we find such machine it will be disqualified.
  • The machine must be powered electrically only. Use of IC engine is not allowed.
  • The electric voltage anywhere in the machine should not be more then 12V DC at any point of time.
  • The robot must not leave behind any of its parts during the run; else it will result in disqualification.
  • Unethical behavior could lead to disqualification. Faculty co-ordinators have all the rights to take final decision for any matter during the event.
  • Judge's decision will be considered final.
  • Certificates of Participation will be given to all the teams that will participate in the event, but not to the teams which get disqualified due to disobeying any of the competition rules.
  • For the robo race will be judged on basis of (in priority):- a. Time to complete the track. b. Number of checkpoints cleared.
  • Organizers reserve the right to interpret and enforce the rules as they see fit. Decisions made by organizers are final.

Welcome to the ANANTAM Tech Fest Esports Event, where the virtual battlegrounds come alive with intense competition, strategy, and skill. Gamers from across the digital realm will converge to showcase their prowess in Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI), Free Fire, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO), and Valorant.   • In the event of a player disconnection, the match will continue as planned.
  • Prizes will be awarded to the top-performing teams in each game.
  • Organizers reserve the right to interpret and enforce the rules as they see fit. Decisions made by organizers are final.

The "Blind Code Challenge" transforms coding into a sensory experience where participants showcase their programming prowess without the luxury of visual aids. This unique challenge requires participants to write given code snippets solely based on auditory or tactile cues. With a focus on precision and speed, participants aim to minimize errors and showcase their coding instincts. The "Blind Code Challenge" goes beyond standard coding competitions, providing a dynamic and sensory-rich experience. Participants not only showcase their coding proficiency but also demonstrate adaptability and precision under unique constraints. This event celebrates the diverse dimensions of coding expertise and the thrill of coding without relying on visual aids.

Rules

  • Open to coding enthusiasts seeking a thrilling and unconventional challenge, the "Blind Code Challenge" invites participants to embrace the excitement of coding without visual cues, adding a layer of complexity to the traditional coding experience.
  • Participants are presented with code snippets or programming problems without the ability to see their screens. They rely solely on auditory or tactile cues to interpret and write the code accurately. The challenge may involve a speed component, with participants vying for the fastest and most error-free solutions.
  • Organizers reserve the right to interpret and enforce the rules as they see fit. Decisions made by organizers are final.

///SYMPOSIUM

ANANT INSIGHT SYMPOSIUM is a series of speaker sessions, consisting of a sequence of presentations and conversations led by eminent speakers from diverse domains, categorized into three themes: technology, administrative/public sector, and non-tech. These sessions cater to diverse interests and career trajectories, offering a valuable opportunity to learn from experts across various fields. They serve as a platform to gain inspiration and uphold our college's dedication to fostering a culture of ongoing learning, innovation, and comprehensive education.

Themes:

  • Technology Theme: Eminent speakers from the tech industry, MNCs, and institutes like CSIR-CEERI and BITS Pilani share their expertise, insights, and experiences related to cutting-edge technology, innovations, and trends.
  • Administrative/Public Sector Theme: Speakers from IAS, IPS, and other services provide valuable perspectives on the administrative and public sectors, discussing policy, governance, and the intersection of technology and the public sphere.
  • Non-Tech Theme: Talks in this category explore diverse topics beyond technology, offering a broader perspective on areas such as leadership, entrepreneurship, and creativity.
